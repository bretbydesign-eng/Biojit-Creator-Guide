Science & Research Reference

21 peer-reviewed studies on Shilajit and Fulvic Acid — all links clickable

These are the clinical studies, randomized controlled trials, and peer-reviewed publications backing the benefits we promote. ⚡ Testosterone & Male Hormones 2 studies
Study 01  ·  Andrologia / Wiley  ·  2016
Pandit et al. — Landmark Testosterone RCT
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. 90 days at 250mg twice daily significantly increased total testosterone, free testosterone, and DHEAS vs. placebo in healthy men aged 45–55. Total testosterone rose ~20%, free testosterone ~19%, DHEAS ~31%.
PubMed Wiley Full Text ResearchGate PDF
Study 02  ·  Andrologia  ·  2010
Biswas et al. — Sperm Quality + Testosterone in Infertile Men
100mg twice daily for 90 days in oligospermic men. Serum testosterone increased +23.5% (P<0.001), total sperm count +61.4%, sperm motility improved significantly, and semen oxidative stress marker (MDA) decreased -18.7%. Hepatic and renal profiles remained unaffected — confirming safety.
PubMed
🦴 Bone Density & Women's Health 3 studies
Study 03  ·  Phytomedicine (RCT)  ·  2022
Pingali & Nutalapati — BMD in Postmenopausal Women
First clinical trial demonstrating shilajit preserves bone mineral density. 48-week randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in 60 postmenopausal women with osteopenia. Dose-dependent reduction in bone turnover markers (CTX-1, BALP, RANKL). OPG increased. MDA (oxidative stress) and hsCRP (inflammation) both significantly decreased vs. placebo. 500mg group showed greater BMD preservation than 250mg group.
PubMed ScienceDirect
Study 04  ·  J. Orthopaedic Surgery & Research  ·  2022
Kangari et al. — Osteogenic Differentiation via Stem Cells
Shilajit acted as an enhancer of alkaline phosphatase (ALP) activity in 3D hydrogel culture and demonstrated osteoinductive properties even in the absence of osteogenic medium. Supports bone formation at a cellular level.
BioMed Central Full Text
Study 05  ·  MDPI Life  ·  2025
Cetin et al. — Shilajit + Xenograft Bone Regeneration
High-dose shilajit (250mg/kg) combined with bovine xenograft significantly increased total antioxidant status, reduced TNF-alpha in a dose-dependent manner, achieved 78.1% new bone area percentage, and lowest fibrous tissue content (9.8%). Demonstrated antioxidative, anti-inflammatory, and osteogenic mechanisms in a rat tibial defect model.
MDPI Life Full Text
💪 Muscle, Strength & Athletic Performance 3 studies
Study 06  ·  J. Int. Society of Sports Nutrition  ·  2019
Keller et al. — Muscular Strength & Collagen Degradation
Double-blind, placebo-controlled trial at University of Nebraska-Lincoln. 63 recreationally active men. 8 weeks at 500mg/day PrimaVie Shilajit promoted retention of maximal muscular strength after fatiguing protocol. High-dose group showed a 29% post-supplementation decrease in serum hydroxyproline (HYP), a biomarker of collagen degradation. Favorable connective tissue adaptations confirmed.
PMC Full Text PubMed
Study 07  ·  Journal of Medicinal Food (Ohio State)  ·  2016
Das et al. — Skeletal Muscle Transcriptome
Oral shilajit supplementation (250mg twice daily, 8 weeks) in obese humans upregulated a cluster of 17 extracellular matrix (ECM)-related genes in skeletal muscle, including: COL1A1, COL1A2, COL5A2, COL6A3, COL14A1 (collagen types), elastin, fibronectin 1, decorin, myoferlin. Effect was highly synergistic with exercise.
PMC Full Text
Study 08  ·  Journal of Dietary Supplements  ·  2024
Neltner et al. — Type 1 Collagen Synthesis Biomarker RCT
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. 35 men received 500mg or 1000mg shilajit daily for 8 weeks. Both groups showed significant increases in serum pro-c1alpha1 — a direct biomarker of new Type 1 collagen synthesis. 84% of the high-dose group and 79% of the low-dose group showed clinically significant growth vs. only 30% in placebo.
ResearchGate
✨ Skin, UV Protection & Collagen 3 studies
Study 09  ·  J. American College of Nutrition  ·  2019
Das et al. — Skin Transcriptome & Microperfusion in Women
Clinical trial (NCT02762032). Middle-aged women received 125 or 250mg shilajit twice daily for 14 weeks. Skin biopsies confirmed upregulation of collagen genes COL1A1, COL5A2, COL14A1 and genes related to blood vessel growth. Skin microcirculation significantly improved at the 250mg dose vs. placebo. Suggests shilajit activates anti-aging mechanisms at the genomic level.
PubMed ResearchGate PDF
Study 10  ·  Int. Journal of Ayurveda Research / PMC  ·  2010
Meena et al. — Shilajit: A Panacea for High-Altitude Problems
Comprehensive review examining shilajit's role in addressing physiological challenges at high altitude — including hypoxia, acute mountain sickness, pulmonary edema, cerebral edema, and high-altitude stress. Shilajit contains fulvic acid and over 84 minerals shown to support oxygen utilization, nutrient transport into deep tissues, and adaptogenic stress responses. Particularly relevant given that authentic Himalayan shilajit is sourced at 13,500–18,000 ft elevation. Recommended dose: 300–500 mg/day.
PMC Full Text PubMed
Study 11  ·  PubMed  ·  2015
Radiation-Induced Ovarian Damage Prevention
First reported study on shilajit's protective effects against radiation-induced ovarian damage in rats. Shilajit decreased expression of p53, Bax, and caspase 3 — thereby blocking apoptotic pathways. Shilajit was found to be especially protective of primordial follicles.

🔬 Fulvic Acid & Cancer / Cell Apoptosis 5 studies
⚠️
Framing reminder
All studies below are in vitro (cell culture) or animal models. Present as "emerging research" only — never as human clinical findings or medical claims.
Study 12  ·  Int. Immunopharmacology  ·  2016
Jayasooriya et al. — FA & Cancer Cell Apoptosis (Fibrosarcoma, Liver, Prostate, Leukemia)
Fulvic acid induced generation of NO and iNOS in RAW 264.7 immune cells via NF-kB activation. FA-conditioned media augmented apoptosis of MCA-102 fibrosarcoma cells. FA-CM also enhanced cell death in Hep3B (liver), LNCaP (prostate), and HL60 (leukemia) human cancer cells. Mechanism: FA stimulates immune-modulating molecules and induces cancer cell apoptosis.
PubMed
Study 13  ·  MDPI Proceedings  ·  2017
Aydin et al. — Fulvic Acid vs. Liver, Colon & Prostate Cancer Cell Lines
Fulvic acid inhibited proliferation of all cancer cell lines tested (Hep3B liver, HT29 colon, PC3 prostate). Hep3B cells were most sensitive with IC50 of 1.58–2.43 µg/µL at 48 hours. FA significantly upregulated apoptotic genes at mRNA levels vs. untreated control.
MDPI Full Text
Study 14  ·  Iranian J. Basic Medical Sciences / PMC  ·  2022
Salehi et al. — Fulvic Acid & Melanoma (Skin Cancer)
Humic acid and fulvic acid tested on A375 human melanoma cell line. Both decreased cell viability dose-dependently (IC50 = 50 µg/mL). Cells were arrested in G0/G1 phase and sub-G1 phase increased — confirming apoptosis induction. Pro-apoptotic effects confirmed via increased BAX/BCL-2 expression ratio. FA also inhibited melanogenesis by decreasing expression of the Tyr gene.
PMC Full Text PubMed
Study 15  ·  European Journal of Cancer Care  ·  2024
Gulcicek et al. — Fulvic Acid & Breast Cancer in SCID Mice
Prophylactic and therapeutic effects of fulvic acid tested in an MCF-7 breast cancer model in SCID mice. In the prophylactic group, no tumor growth was observed in most of the mammary tissue. Bcl-2 and Bax (antiapoptotic/proapoptotic proteins) were significantly reduced by prophylactic FA application. Suggests FA may have preventive potential against tumor formation.
Wiley Full Text
Study 16  ·  Discover Oncology / Springer Nature  ·  2025
Fulvic Acid & Ovarian Cancer
FA suppressed cell proliferation, migration, and invasion in SK-OV-3 and OVCAR3 ovarian cancer cells. Mechanism identified: upregulation of CYP1A1 (cytochrome P450 family 1 subfamily A member 1). RNA sequencing confirmed differentially expressed mRNAs. First study documenting FA antitumor effects in ovarian cancer.
PubMed Springer Full Text PMC Full Text
🧠 Cognitive Health & Alzheimer's Research 2 studies
Study 17  ·  Journal of Alzheimer's Disease  ·  2011
Carrasco-Gallardo et al. — Fulvic Acid Inhibits Tau Protein Aggregation
The foundational Alzheimer's / fulvic acid study. Tau protein aggregation forming paired helical filaments (PHFs) — the hallmark tangles of Alzheimer's disease — was inhibited by fulvic acid in vitro. FA affected the length and morphology of tau fibrils, and also disaggregated pre-formed filaments. Mechanism: FA acts at the hydrophobic level, interfering with sticky bonds holding protein clumps together.
PubMed
Study 18  ·  Quality in Sport Journal  ·  2025
Kazmierkowicz et al. — Systematic Review: Fulvic Acid & Alzheimer's Disease
Systematic review across PubMed, Google Scholar, and ResearchGate. Conclusion: Fulvic acid, due to its anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties, shows promising potential in slowing cognitive decline and protecting against neurodegeneration in Alzheimer's disease. Authors call for further clinical trials.
Quality in Sport Journal

The most up-to-date review (2025) covering all clinical studies on shilajit. Covers mechanisms of action across: testosterone production, collagen synthesis, bone health, anti-inflammatory effects, antioxidant activity, and safety. Synthesizes preclinical and clinical trial data. Identifies NF-kB and Nrf2/HO-1 as key signaling pathways.

IJRPR — Shilajit & Post-Exercise Muscle Recovery: Mechanisms Review
Comprehensive review of shilajit's muscle recovery mechanisms: (1) Mitochondrial bioenergetics — improved electron transport chain function and ATP synthesis; (2) Modulation of inflammatory cascade and oxidative stress via antioxidant constituents; (3) Anabolic support through testosterone and DHEA upregulation; (4) Promotion of ECM integrity — simultaneously reducing collagen degradation and upregulating collagen synthesis. Clinical trials using 250–500mg/day confirmed improved fatigue resistance and strength retention.

Winkler — Therapeutic Potential of Fulvic Acid in Chronic Inflammatory Diseases and Diabetes
Comprehensive review examining fulvic acid's therapeutic mechanisms across chronic inflammatory conditions and type 2 diabetes. Fulvic acid demonstrated inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokines, modulation of immune response pathways, and improvement in insulin sensitivity markers. Authors identify FA's multi-target action as a key advantage over single-molecule drugs, with a strong safety profile supporting its use as a natural adjunct therapy.

Multi-Stage Purification — No Shortcuts
Raw shilajit goes through a thorough purification process using lab-tested purified water. We use natural purification agents including activated charcoal, moringa seeds, and quartz crystal rocks to gently extract unwanted heavy metals and impurities — no harsh chemicals, no industrial processing. The entire process is kept under 20°C to preserve the raw, unpasteurized integrity of the shilajit.
🔬
200nm UVC Sterilization — Then Sun-Dried in Stainless Steel
Before the final drying stage, the purified shilajit is treated with a gentle 200-nanometre UVC sterilization — effective and non-invasive. It is then sun-dried in stainless steel trays using traditional methods. The Drops and Resin formats differ only in viscosity: the Drops are sun-dried for a shorter period, giving a more liquid, easy-to-dose tincture. The Resin is sun-dried longer — the original, thicker, traditional form.
🧪
USA Third-Party Lab Tested — Every Batch, Full Panel
Every single batch is independently tested by US third-party laboratories. Testing covers: fulvic acid content, heavy metals, microbials, pesticides, mold, E. coli, and salmonella. We publish the results. Total transparency — no guesswork, nothing hidden.
✅
100% Pure — Zero Fillers, Zero Additives
Both the Drops and Resin are the same 100% organic, natural, raw, pure Himalayan shilajit — just in different viscosities. No fillers, no preservatives, no artificial ingredients. GMO-free, vegan-friendly, and gluten-free. What's on the label is exactly what's in the jar or bottle.
